Core principle: divergence is a signal, not noise. DNS uses LLMs as perspective generators, each shaped by architecture, training data, and alignment. Divergence is classified along four axes: Substantive – different facts or estimates, resolved by external validation; Normative – different values or risk tolerances, documented as open questions; Didactic – different explanations with same claim, ignored in synthesis; Architectural – differences traceable to model design, noted but not merged. This typology prevents false consensus and makes the synthesis auditable under Articles 13-15.
