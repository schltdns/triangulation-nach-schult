# 📢 Open Call for Co-Authors & Endorsers: Help Shape the Future of Epistemic AI Evaluation (arXiv cs.AI)

## Join the Quest for Epistemic Reliability in AI

I am preparing a submission to **arXiv (cs.AI)** for the **Divergence Navigation System (DNS)** — a **divergence-based evaluation layer** that quantifies semantic disagreement ($\Delta_{div}$) between AI models to **reveal blind spots, bias, and alignment tunneling**.

**DNS is not just another multi-agent framework.** Unlike AutoGen or CrewAI, it **doesn’t optimize for consensus** — it **maps and interprets disagreement** to make epistemic uncertainty actionable.

> **Note:** DNS operates in **epistemic space** (semantic disagreement), not physical robotics or navigation.

---

## 🎯 Why DNS Matters for AI Research & Governance

DNS is a **model-agnostic, training-free evaluation layer** designed to:

### 1. Measure Semantic Divergence ($\Delta_{div}$)
Quantifies disagreement between model outputs to expose blind spots that consensus-based systems hide.

*Example:* If 4 LLMs (e.g., GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek) answer a question about climate policy, DNS maps their semantic dispersion to show **where and why models disagree**.
Δdiv = 1 - (Jaccard + Cosine Similarity)/2 // Simplified example

### 2. Implement Falsification (P1–P8)
A structured protocol for hypothesis testing, power‑layer analysis, operator reflection, and versioned archiving, inspired by Popperian epistemology.

*Use case:* Governance teams can audit AI decisions by tracking which hypotheses fail under divergence analysis.

### 3. Open Source (Apache‑2.0)
Designed for maximum integration — from local LLM deployments (e.g., Ollama) to cloud‑based multi‑agent systems.

*Community:* **Active and growing interest** (see [GitHub](https://github.com/schltdns/divergence-navigation-system)).

**Impact:**  
- **AI Safety:** Detects hallucinations before deployment.  
- **Governance:** Provides auditable disagreement maps for compliance.  
- **Research:** Enables automated Delphi studies in hours, not months.

## ⚖️ Testable Claim: Can $\Delta_{div}$ Predict Blind Spots?

DNS’s core hypothesis:

> *"Semantic divergence ($\Delta_{div}$) between AI models correlates with expert-identified blind spots across domains."*

**We invite falsification attempts.**  
- Have you found cases where **high $\Delta_{div}$ doesn’t indicate a blind spot**? Help us refine the metric.  
- Can you **break the P1–P8 protocol**? Help us improve it.  
- **Stress-test the code** ([GitHub](https://github.com/schltdns/divergence-navigation-system)) or **challenge the whitepaper** ([Zenodo](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19513073)).
