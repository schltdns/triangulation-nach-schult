**Run:** dns_v2.1_cognitive_safety  
**Date:** 2026-04-18  

## Status

This case study documents an **epistemic validation** through multi‑model triangulation. It is **not** an empirical validation. No external, independent study has yet tested the cognitive safety hypothesis.

## Types of Validation – Comparison with Labour Market 2030

| Validation type | Labour Market 2030 | Cognitive Safety |
|----------------|--------------------|------------------|
| **Epistemic (model consensus)** | Yes (Δdiv = 0.6256, high divergence) | Yes (Δdiv = 0.787, high structural divergence) |
| **External data (IAB, WEF, McKinsey, etc.)** | Yes – used for falsification criteria | **None available** – no independent studies on DNS and social evaluation anxiety |
| **Peer‑reviewed publication** | No (preprint only) | No (preprint only) |
| **Empirical pilot** | No | Planned  |

## Why No External Validation Currently Exists

The hypothesis is novel. No prior research has operationalised “low‑social‑risk cognitive environments” using LLM divergence as a signal. Therefore, no existing datasets or studies can be used for external validation.

---

## What This Case Study Does Provide

- **Internal consistency:** All six models independently confirm the hypothesis (with documented caveats).
- **Falsifiability:** Clear falsification criteria are defined (see `02_thresholds.md`).
- **Reproducibility:** The full protocol (P1–P6) is documented and can be replicated.
- **Divergence transparency:** High Δdiv (0.787) is documented and interpreted as structural, not substantive.

## Conclusion on Validation Status

> **This case study establishes a plausible, falsifiable hypothesis with strong model consensus. It does not provide external empirical validation. That remains future work.**
