
# 05_synthesis.md – Synthesis of Model Responses

**Run:** dns_v2.1_cognitive_safety

## Core Consensus (All Models)

The following statements are supported by all six models (ChatGPT, Claude, DeepSeek, Gemini, Grok, Copilot):

1. **Mechanism:** DNS shifts social evaluation from the synchronous exploration phase to an asynchronous, text‑based, structured space. It does not eliminate social evaluation but postpones and restructures it.

2. **Target group:** Individuals with high sensitivity to social evaluation – including many neurodivergent persons (autism spectrum, ADHD) and strongly introverted individuals – benefit most from this shift.

3. **Effect:** For this target group, DNS can lead to increased willingness to explore uncertain problems, deeper hypothesis formation, and higher tolerance for contradiction as an epistemic signal.

4. **Tool character:** DNS is a tool, not an autopilot. The quality of the synthesis depends on the operator (human‑in‑the‑loop). DNS itself does not fail; only the operator can fail (through drift, bias, overload, or incorrect relevance filtering).

## Conditionally Supported Statements (with caveats)

| Statement | Supporting models | Caveat / Dissent |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Universal applicability | Gemini, Grok, Claude | ChatGPT and DeepSeek note that effectiveness varies; not everyone profits equally. |
| Operator failure as exclusive error source | DeepSeek, Gemini, Grok, Copilot | ChatGPT warns that design can amplify or dampen operator failure – not purely operator error. |
| Neurodivergence as the core origin | Grok | Claude clarifies: origin was geopolitical curiosity; son is an application case, not the origin. |

## Final Formulation of the Hypothesis (Approved by All Models)

> **DNS creates a cognitive space with minimal social costs. For individuals whose thinking is inhibited by social evaluation anxiety – including many neurodivergent (autism spectrum, ADHD) and strongly introverted persons – this can lead to more courage, deeper exploration, and more systematic research.**

## Guiding Principle (Claude)

> *"DNS emerged because curious people stop being curious when they fear social costs."*

## Remaining Open Questions (for Empirical Testing)

- Is a minimal operator training sufficient for reliable application in schools?
- How does DNS compare to other low‑social‑risk methods (e.g., private journaling, anonymous online forums)?
