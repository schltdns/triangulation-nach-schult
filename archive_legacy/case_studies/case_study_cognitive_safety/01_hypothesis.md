# 01_hypothesis.md – Cognitive Safety: DNS as a Low‑Social‑Risk Environment

**Run:** dns_v2.1_cognitive_safety  
**Date:** 2026-04-18  

## Hypothesis

The Divergence Navigation System (DNS) creates a **low‑social‑risk cognitive environment** by shifting social evaluation from the synchronous exploration phase to an asynchronous, text‑based, structured space.  

For individuals whose thinking is inhibited by **social evaluation anxiety** – including many neurodivergent persons (autism spectrum, ADHD) and strongly introverted individuals – this can lead to:

- Increased willingness to explore uncertain problems  
- Deeper hypothesis formation and iterative reasoning  
- Higher tolerance for contradiction as an epistemic signal  

**Core mechanism:** DNS does not eliminate social evaluation – it postpones and restructures it. The human operator remains in control (P6). The quality of the synthesis depends on the operator, not on the system alone.

## Research Questions

1. Does DNS measurably reduce perceived social costs during exploration?  
2. Can DNS increase the number and depth of hypotheses generated for a complex, uncertain problem?  
3. Is the effect stronger for individuals with high sensitivity to social evaluation compared to low‑sensitivity individuals?

## Scope

This case study documents the **epistemic validation** of the hypothesis through multi‑model triangulation (ChatGPT, Claude, DeepSeek, Gemini, Grok, Copilot) using the full DNS protocol (P1–P6). It is not an empirical study. Empirical testing is reserved for a later stage (e.g., pilot with Montessori school or IHK trainees).

## Expected Outputs

- Independent confirmation or falsification of the hypothesis by each of the six models  
- Divergence map highlighting differences in framing, terminology, and structural emphasis  
- Operator decisions resolving normative and substantive contradictions  
- Δdiv calculation based on actual model outputs (see `04_divergence_map.md` and `delta_div_results.json`)  
- A final synthesised statement that can serve as a hypothesis for empirical testing

## Falsification Criteria (preliminary)

The hypothesis would be falsified if:

- At least three models state that DNS does **not** reduce social evaluation anxiety  
- Models agree that asynchronous, text‑based interaction has **no meaningful effect** on exploration behaviour  
- Models reject the premise that social evaluation anxiety is a significant barrier for neurodivergent or introverted individuals

These criteria are refined in `02_thresholds.md`.

## Relation to the Labour Market 2030 Case Study

The labour market study demonstrated DNS’s ability to handle high‑divergence, policy‑relevant questions (Δdiv = 0.6256). The present study applies the same protocol to a psychological/didactic hypothesis. Comparing both studies illustrates DNS’s domain‑agnostic nature.
