> *Full response as provided on 2025-04-06 (condensed to A–G structure)*

### A) Economic Dimensions
- **Productivity gains:** AI acts as a General Purpose Technology. Short-term productivity paradox; long‑term +1–3% p.a.
- **Net job balance:** Not pure job loss, but substitution + transformation + creation. Historically net positive, but risk of time lag.
- **Wages & dispersion:** Polarisation – high‑skilled benefit (skill multiplier), middle qualifications under pressure, superstar effects.
- **High‑automation sectors:** Administration, accounting, customer service, transport (long‑term), manufacturing (medium), care/education (low).
- **New jobs 2030:** AI trainer, prompt engineer, human‑AI interaction designer, data ethicist, automation architect, hybrid jobs.
- **Macro effects:** Significant GDP growth, inflation dampening through efficiency, capital returns rise faster.

### B) Technological Dimensions
- **Automation level:** Knowledge work more affected than previously assumed. Automation of language, images, decision processes.
- **Agent systems:** Transition from tools to autonomous agents (plan, execute, collaborate). Multimodality integrates text, image, audio, video.
- **Cooperation vs. substitution:** Short‑term augmentation, long‑term partial substitution. Centaur model (human+AI outperforms each alone).
- **Skill shift:** *Disappearing:* routine analysis, basic programming, standardised office work. *Emerging:* systems thinking, problem solving, AI steering & interpretation, social & creative skills.
- **Diffusion speed:** Faster than previous innovations (cloud, APIs). Bottleneck: organisation & culture, not technology.

### C) Social Dimensions
- **Social mobility:** Opportunity through democratisation of knowledge, risk of new inequality (access to AI).
- **Inequality & polarisation:** Increase likely without political countermeasures. Winner‑takes‑most.
- **Age risks:** Younger benefit through adaptability, older (50+) higher risk of skill obsolescence.
- **Regional differences:** Cities benefit more (tech clusters), rural regions at risk of job loss. Global: US/China AI leadership, Europe regulation + skills shortage.
- **Mental health:** Positive: relief from monotonous work. Negative: uncertainty, loss of control, always‑on pressure.

### D) Education & Training
- **Future of VET:** Stronger integration of digital skills and AI tools. Practical relevance remains key.
- **Upskilling/reskilling:** Lifelong learning becomes standard, micro‑credentials, modular education.
- **Future‑proof skills:** Analytical thinking, creativity, social intelligence, adaptability.
- **AI‑supported learning:** Personalised AI tutors, simulations & immersive learning environments, real‑time feedback.
- **Dual training:** Competitive advantage (especially Germany). Combination of theory and practice becomes even more valuable.

### E) Policy & Regulation
- **Working time:** 4‑day week possible as productivity dividend, job sharing under automation pressure.
- **Migration:** Short‑term critical against skills shortage, long‑term partially substitutable by AI.
- **AI regulation:** Balance between innovation and protection. Too strict → competitive disadvantage, too loose → social risks.
- **Welfare state:** Debated: basic income, negative income tax, wage subsidies. Focus: cushioning transition phases.

### F) Corporate Strategy
- **Re‑skilling vs. layoffs:** Successful companies invest in internal training, transformation rather than reduction.
- **AI‑driven organisation:** Flatter hierarchies, more autonomous teams, data‑driven decisions.
- **Leadership & management:** Shift from control to coaching. Leaders must understand AI.
- **Cost‑benefit:** Short‑term high investment, long‑term enormous scaling effects.
- **Work processes:** End‑to‑end automation, humans intervene only in exceptions.

### G) Divergence Hotspots
- Job loss vs. job change: Optimistic – transformation dominates; Pessimistic – structural unemployment.
- Speed of automation: Scenario A gradual (adaptable) vs. Scenario B exponential (disruptive).
- Role of vocational training: Key to adaptation vs. risk of outdated curricula.
- Winners & losers: Tech, education, healthcare vs. routine‑intensive services.
- Scenarios: Optimistic – welfare gains for many; Pessimistic – inequality + social tensions.
- Normative assessments: AI as opportunity (efficiency, innovation) vs. risk (loss of control, inequality).

**ChatGPT short conclusion:** The labour market 2030 will not be shaped primarily by "human vs. machine" but by "human with machine vs. human without machine". Decisive: speed of adaptation, quality of education systems, political design.
